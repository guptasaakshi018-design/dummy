In the age of sustainable development, the relationship between business transactions and environmental stewardship is becoming more and more important. With an emphasis on corporate accountability within Indian and International legal framework, this paper investigates how environmental governance responsibilities are incorporated into mergers and acquisitions (M&A). Regulatory agencies are placing more stringent requirements on business to guarantee environmental compliance throughout the transaction lifecycles as environment concerns around the world grow. The need of due diligence in fulfilling environmental requirements, responsibility transfers and compliance concerns are all given special consideration. The study examines important laws including the Environment (Protection) Act of 1986, the Companies Act of 2013 and the National Green Tribunal Act of 2010 in addition to global standards like the principle of the Paris Agreement and the OECD Guidelines.
